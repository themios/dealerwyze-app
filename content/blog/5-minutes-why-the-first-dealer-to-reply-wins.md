5 Minutes: Why the First Dealer to Reply Wins (And How to Be That Dealer)

The industry benchmark for lead response isn’t “same day” or “first thing tomorrow.” It’s **under five minutes**. Buyers who inquire about a used car are often texting or emailing multiple dealers at once. The first one to answer clearly, with the right vehicle and a real next step, gets the conversation. Everyone else gets silence - and then a ghosted thread.

Small lots don’t lose because their inventory is worse or their prices are wrong. They lose because leads land in Gmail, AutoTrader, CarGurus, and voicemail, and there’s no single place to see who just came in and act fast. By the time you find the right email, remember which car they wanted, and type a reply, five minutes have become five hours. The deal is already elsewhere.

**How to be the dealer who wins:** You need one place where every lead lands the moment it arrives, tied to the exact vehicle they’re interested in, with one-tap templates so you can send a personal, relevant reply in under a minute. DealerWyze does exactly that. Connect Gmail (or any IMAP inbox), and CarGurus, AutoTrader, Cars For Sale, and other lead emails import automatically - no copy-paste. Each lead becomes a contact linked to the car from your synced inventory, so the link in your reply goes to the *actual* listing, not a generic lot page. Pick a first-contact template (e.g. virtual appointment + delivery + financing), hit send. First response in three minutes is realistic, not heroic. The app records when you replied and tracks response time across SMS, email, and call, so you see green (under 5 min), yellow (5–30), or red (over 30) in your analytics. You’re not guessing; you’re measuring.

DealerWyze is built for the lot: mobile-first, no six-week rollout, no IT department. It’s the platform that makes the five-minute rule achievable for independent dealers - so you stop losing to the dealer who responded at 10:01.
