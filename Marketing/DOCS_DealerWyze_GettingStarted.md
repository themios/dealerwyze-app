# DealerWyze — Getting Started (Plain-Language Help)

**Audience:** Dealership staff who will use the app every day. No software background required.

This doc explains the main ideas and where to find things. For full training, use the Staff Training Guide in this folder.

---

## What DealerWyze is for

DealerWyze is a single place where your dealership’s leads and customer conversations live. When someone contacts you by email (e.g. from CarGurus or your website), by text, or by phone, that contact and their messages can show up here. You see who they are, which car they asked about, and what to do next — and you can reply quickly with the right information and the right link.

In other words: **one list for everyone who might buy, the car they’re interested in, and the next step (call back, send link, set appointment).**

---

## Main areas of the app

- **Today** — Your daily to-do list. It shows new leads, people you said you’d call back, overdue follow-ups, and today’s appointments. Start here each day and work from the top.
- **Leads (or Customers)** — The full list of people who have shown interest. You can search by name or phone. Click a row to open that person’s record and see all messages and history.
- **Inventory** — The cars your dealership has for sale. The app can sync this from your website so the list and links stay up to date. When you send a message, the app can insert the link to the exact car the customer asked about.
- **Analytics (or Reports)** — Numbers about leads, response time, and sales. Managers and admins usually see this; your role may see a simplified view.

---

## How to reply to a lead quickly

1. Open **Today** or **Leads** and find the person (e.g. a new lead).
2. Open their record (click or tap the row).
3. Choose to send an **SMS** or **Email** (depending on how they contacted you or how you want to reply).
4. Pick a **template** (e.g. “First contact – virtual look + delivery”). The app fills in their name, the car, the price, and the link to that car’s listing.
5. Check the preview, add a line if you want, then send.

The goal is to reply in under 60 seconds when you can. Templates let you send a personal-feeling message without typing everything from scratch.

---

## How texting works (business number)

Your dealership can have a dedicated phone number for texts. When a customer texts that number:

- The message appears in DealerWyze on that customer’s record (matched by phone number).
- You reply from inside the app. Your reply is sent from the same business number.
- All texts with that customer stay in their timeline so you have the full conversation in one place.

You don’t need to use your personal phone for customer texts. Everything stays in the app.

---

## How to log a call and set a callback

After you make or receive a call (especially if you started the call from the app), a screen may pop up: **“How did the call go?”**

- Choose what happened: **Answered**, **No Answer**, or **Left Voicemail**.
- Add a short note if something important was said (e.g. “Coming in Saturday 10am”).
- If you promised to call back, use the option to **schedule a follow-up** (e.g. “In 2 hours” or “Tomorrow 9am”). That follow-up will show up on your Today list so you don’t forget.

If you don’t want to fill it out right then, you can close the pop-up with the **X**. You can add a note or a task later from the customer’s record.

---

## What “response time” means

**Response time** is how long it took from when the lead came in to when someone first replied (by text, email, or call). Many dealers aim for under 60 seconds. The app can show you and your manager how fast you’re replying so you can improve. Faster, clear first replies usually mean more show-ups and more deals.

---

## If something doesn’t look right

- **Wrong car or missing link:** Tell your manager. They can check that the inventory page URL is set correctly and run a sync so the app’s vehicle list and links match your website.
- **Can’t send a text to a customer:** The customer may have opted out of texts (e.g. replied STOP). The app blocks sending marketing texts to them to stay within the rules. Your manager can confirm.
- **Missing lead or message:** If a lead usually comes by email, make sure the app is still connected to that email (your manager or admin handles that in Settings). If it came in by text, check that it’s the business number the app uses.
