## 1. Why DealerWyze and what you'll learn

**The problem we're solving**

Buyers contact you in many ways: email (Gmail, listing-site notifications), website forms, texts, and phone calls. Without one central place, it's easy to:

- Miss a lead because it was buried in email
- Reply late and lose the deal to a competitor who answered first
- Forget who asked about which car and send the wrong link or info
- Lose track of who you said you'd call back

**What DealerWyze does**

- **One list:** Every inquiry (from email, CarGurus, AutoTrader, your website, texts, and calls) lands in one place: the app.
- **One record per person:** Each lead is a customer record. The app ties them to the exact vehicle they asked about so you never send the wrong car or price.
- **One "Today" list:** You see who needs attention now — new leads, callbacks, follow-ups, appointments — ordered by urgency, not by which email arrived first.
- **Reply in a few taps:** Saved messages (templates) fill in the customer's name, the car, and the price. You send a personal-feeling message without typing it from scratch.
- **One business number for texts:** Customer texts that number; you see and reply from the app. All threads stay with the right customer and the right car.

**What you'll learn in this guide**

You will learn how to: find and open any lead; use the Today list to prioritize your day; send a first reply in under five minutes using templates; text customers from the app; log calls and choose outcomes; understand response time and basic analytics; and use optional tools (daily brief, lead scanner, after-hours voice, BHPH) if your dealership has them turned on.

**Check your understanding**

- In one sentence, what is the main benefit of DealerWyze for your daily work?
- Name three places a lead can come from before it shows up in the app.

## 2. Where leads come from and how they land in the app

**Channels that feed into DealerWyze**

| Source | How it gets into the app | What you see |
|--------|---------------------------|--------------|
| **Gmail / connected email** | The app is connected to the dealership's lead email (e.g. Gmail). New messages that look like leads (e.g. from CarGurus, AutoTrader, or your website) are read and turned into a customer + activity. | A new row in Leads with the person's name, contact info, and often the vehicle they asked about. An activity of type "email" with the content. |
| **Listing sites (CarGurus, AutoTrader, etc.)** | Many of these send an email to you when someone inquires. That email is what the app reads — so the lead still "comes from email" from the app's point of view. | Same as above: new customer, vehicle linked if we can match it, and an email activity. |
| **Website form** | If your website sends lead notifications to the same connected email, they're treated like any other lead email. | New customer with the form details and vehicle interest if provided. |
| **SMS (text)** | When someone texts your dealership's business number, the app receives the message and attaches it to that customer (matched by phone number) or creates a new customer. | An activity of type "sms", direction "inbound", on the customer record. You see it in the customer's timeline and can reply from the app. |
| **Phone call** | Inbound calls can be logged manually or, if you use the after-hours AI voice feature, the AI can create a lead from the call. | An activity of type "call" and/or a new or updated customer with notes from the call. |
| **Manual / paste / scanner** | A manager or you can add a lead by pasting info or using the Lead Scanner (photo of a handwritten lead or PDF). | A new customer created by a person, with whatever details were entered or extracted. |

**Why this matters for you**

You don't have to check five different places. Open DealerWyze and look at **Leads** or **Today** — everything that's come in is there. Your job is to act on it, not to hunt for it.

## 3. The Leads list and customer records

**What the Leads list is**

The Leads list (often under "Leads" or "Customers" in the app menu) is the full list of people who have shown interest — from any source. Each row is one customer (one person). You may see columns like name, phone, last activity, lead source, and assignment.

**Filters and assignment**

- **If your dealership uses assignment:** You may see only the leads assigned to you, or you may see "All" with an option to filter to "Mine." Use the filter that matches your role so you're not overwhelmed.
- **Archived:** Some leads are archived (e.g. sold, lost, or duplicate). They're usually hidden from the main list unless you choose "Show archived."

**Opening a customer record**

Click or tap a row to open that customer's detail view. You'll see:

- **Header:** Name, phone(s), email, and often a primary vehicle of interest.
- **Timeline (activity):** Every email, SMS, and call in order. This is the full conversation history.
- **Vehicles:** Cars they're linked to (the ones they asked about). You can send a link to the exact listing from here.
- **Actions:** Buttons to send an SMS, send an email, log a call, add a task, or schedule a follow-up.

**Why the vehicle link matters**

When you reply, you want to send a link to *the car they asked about*, not "all our cars." The app stores that link (from your inventory) so your template can insert it. That way the buyer gets the right car and price in one message.

- Where do you see the full history of emails and texts for a customer?
- Why is it important that each lead is tied to a specific vehicle?

## 4. The Today list: your daily action list

**What the Today list is**

Today is your prioritized to-do list. It doesn't show "every lead" — it shows *who needs action now*: new leads, people you said you'd call back, overdue follow-ups, and today's appointments. Items are ordered by urgency (e.g. new lead first, then overdue callbacks, then appointments).

**What you'll see**

- **New leads** that haven't been contacted yet (or that need a first reply).
- **Tasks** such as "Call back [Name]" or "Follow up with [Name]" with a due date. Overdue items are usually highlighted.
- **Appointments** for today.
- **Voice calls** that came in and need a disposition (if your store uses the after-call flow).

**How to use it**

1. Start your day by opening Today.
2. Work from the top down. The first items are usually the most time-sensitive.
3. When you complete an action (e.g. you text the lead or log a call), the app can create the next step (e.g. "Call back in 2 hours" or "Tomorrow 9am"). That next step shows up on Today so you don't forget.

**Adding a task from a customer**

When you're on a customer record, you can add a task (e.g. "Call back Friday 10am"). That task will appear on Today for the right day. So your "to-do" is always tied to a person and a next step.

## 5. Replying fast: templates and personalization

**Why speed matters**

Research shows that the first dealer to reply clearly and quickly usually gets the conversation — and often the sale. DealerWyze is built so you can send a relevant, personal-feeling reply in under five minutes.

**What templates are**

Templates are saved messages for common situations, for example:

- First contact (someone just asked about a car)
- Virtual walkthrough / video
- Delivery options
- Financing / BHPH

When you choose a template, the app fills in placeholders such as:

- **{firstName}** — customer's first name  
- **{vehicle}** — year, make, model (and sometimes trim)  
- **{price}** — listing price  
- **{link}** — link to that specific car's listing  

So the customer sees: "Hi Marcus, the 2009 Acura MDX at $7,495 is available. Here's the link: [correct link]." You didn't type that — you picked the template and sent it.

**How to send a template**

1. Open the customer.
2. Choose SMS or Email (depending on how you're replying).
3. Open the template picker and select the right template (e.g. "First contact – virtual look + delivery").
4. Review the preview. The placeholders should already be filled with that customer's name and the right car/price/link.
5. Edit if you want to add a sentence, then send.

**When to customize**

Templates cover most first replies. If the customer asked a specific question (e.g. "Is the title clean?"), add a short line or send a follow-up message with the answer. The goal is fast *and* relevant.

## 6. Two-way texting from the business number

**How it works**

Your dealership has (or can set up) a business phone number used for texts. When a customer texts that number:

- The message appears in DealerWyze on that customer's record (matched by phone number).
- You reply from the app. Your reply is sent from the same business number, so the conversation stays in one thread on their phone.

All texting with customers happens inside the app — no switching to your personal phone or a separate texting tool.

**Where to see and send texts**

- **On the customer record:** You'll see an SMS section or timeline with inbound and outbound messages. Use the "Send SMS" or message box to reply.
- **Templates:** You can use the same templates for SMS so your first text reply is fast and includes the right car and link.

**Opt-out and compliance**

If a customer replies STOP (or similar), the app records that they opted out. You cannot send them further marketing or reminder texts. The app will not let you send to that number for promotional messages; this is to stay within texting laws (TCPA). Payment reminders for BHPH may have different rules; your manager can clarify.

**Check your understanding**

- When a customer texts the business number, where do you see it and reply from?
- What should you do if a customer has opted out of texts?

## 7. Logging calls and the After-Call screen

**Why we log calls**

Every call you make or receive should be recorded on the customer's record. That way the team sees the full history, and you can set a follow-up task (e.g. "Call back in 2 hours" or "Tomorrow 9am") so nothing falls through the cracks.

**When the After-Call screen appears**

After you finish a call that you started from the app (e.g. by tapping the customer's phone number), a pop-up may appear: **"How did the call go?"** You're asked to:

- Choose an **outcome:** Answered, No Answer, or Left Voicemail.
- Optionally add **notes** (e.g. what you discussed).
- Optionally **schedule a follow-up** (in 2 hours, tomorrow 9am, or a custom date/time).

**What to do**

- Select the outcome that happened. This keeps analytics accurate.
- Add a line of notes if something important was said (e.g. "Coming in Saturday 10am").
- If you promised to call back, use the follow-up option so it appears on Today.

**Closing without saving**

If the pop-up appears but you don't want to log anything right then, use the **X** to close it. The call may still be logged as an activity; the outcome and follow-up just won't be set. You can add notes or a task later from the customer record if needed.

## 8. Analytics and response time: what gets measured

**What the app tracks**

- **Leads:** How many came in, by source (e.g. CarGurus, email, voice), and over what period.
- **Response time:** How quickly the first reply was sent after the lead came in (e.g. under 5 minutes, 5–30 minutes, over 30 minutes). Many dealers aim for under five minutes.
- **SMS:** How many messages were sent and how many customers replied (reply rate).
- **Voice:** Call volume, average duration, and estimated cost if applicable.
- **Funnel:** How many leads are at each stage (new, contacted, engaged, appointment set, showed, sold).
- **Revenue:** Units sold and total revenue in the period (if your store uses this).

**Where you see it**

Managers and admins typically see the full **Analytics** (or Reports) view. You may see a simplified view or just your own stats (e.g. your response time). Ask your manager what’s available for your role.

**Why it matters for you**

Your response time and follow-up rate affect whether leads turn into appointments and sales. The app makes this visible so you can improve. Fast, clear first replies and reliable follow-ups usually mean more show-ups and more deals.

## 9. Inventory sync: keeping cars and links correct

**What inventory sync does**

DealerWyze can pull your current inventory from your dealership website (the "cars for sale" or "inventory" page). It:

- Creates or updates vehicle records in the app (year, make, model, price, stock number, listing URL).
- When a lead asks about a specific car, the app can attach the correct listing link so your template sends the right URL.
- When a car is sold or removed from the website, the app can mark it sold or remove it from active inventory so you don’t send a link to a car that’s gone.

**What you need to do**

Usually nothing. Someone in management sets the inventory page URL in Settings. After that, a sync runs (automatically or when triggered) so the app’s vehicle list and links stay in line with the website. You just use the templates and links the app gives you.

**If a car or link seems wrong**

Tell your manager. They can check that the inventory page URL is correct and run a sync. Rarely, a car might be added manually if it’s not on the website yet.

## 10. Optional features: brief, scanner, voice, BHPH

**Daily brief (Dealer Brief)**

If enabled, the app can show a short daily summary: how many new leads, how many appointments today, and any overdue tasks. Use it to start the day with the big picture before you dive into Today.

**Lead Scanner**

If enabled, you or a manager can take a photo of a handwritten lead (e.g. a sticky note with name, phone, and car) or upload a PDF. The app uses AI to read it and create a customer with that info so those leads don’t get lost.

**After-hours voice (AI)**

If enabled, when someone calls outside business hours, an AI can answer, ask what car they’re interested in and how to reach them, and create a lead with a transcript. You see it the next morning and can call back. You don’t have to be on 24/7 to capture the lead.

**BHPH (Buy Here Pay Here)**

If your store finances cars and collects payments, the app can track loans, send payment reminders by text, and keep records. Payment reminders respect opt-out (STOP) so the dealership stays compliant. If you’re in BHPH, your manager will explain how your role uses this.

## 11. Daily workflow and best practices

**Suggested daily workflow**

1. **Start with Today.** Open the Today list and see what’s urgent: new leads, overdue callbacks, appointments.
2. **Handle new leads first.** Reply within five minutes when possible. Use a template, add the right car link, and send. If you need to call, log the call and set a follow-up if you didn’t reach them.
3. **Work through callbacks and tasks.** Use Today to drive the order. When you complete something, add the next step (e.g. "Call back Friday") so it appears on the right day.
4. **Check Leads for anything not on Today.** Sometimes a lead comes in and doesn’t create a task yet. Scan Leads (or your assigned slice) for "new" or "contacted" and make sure no one is waiting.
5. **End the day with a quick look at Tomorrow.** See what’s due tomorrow so you’re ready in the morning.

**Best practices**

- **Reply fast.** First response under five minutes when you can. Use templates so speed doesn’t mean generic messages.
- **Use the right link.** Always send the link to the specific car they asked about. The app provides it; use it.
- **Log every call.** Outcome + short notes + follow-up if needed. That keeps the team and analytics accurate.
- **Don’t let Today get stale.** Complete tasks or reschedule them. If you can’t reach someone, set a new callback instead of leaving it overdue.
- **Respect opt-out.** If a customer has opted out of SMS, don’t send them marketing texts. The app will block it; if you see a warning, stop.

**Check your understanding**

- Why should you log every call with an outcome and optional follow-up?
