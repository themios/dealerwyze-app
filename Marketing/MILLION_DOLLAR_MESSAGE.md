# Uncover Your Million-Dollar Message (for DealerWyze)

Use this framework **with ChatGPT** (or Claude) to turn your experience, your product, and what people actually want into one clear message that sells. Your multimillion-dollar message is already inside you and inside this application—this guide helps you pull it out.

**How to use this doc:** Run each step in order. Paste the **Application context** (Section 1) into every prompt so the AI knows DealerWyze and your audience. If you’ve been using the same AI for a while, it already has your history; the context block grounds it in the product.

---

## 1. Application context (paste into every prompt)

Copy this block into each prompt below so the model stays focused on DealerWyze and your audience:

```
Product: DealerWyze — a CRM built for small used-car dealerships.

Core value: One place for every lead, every car, and every next step. Reply fast, stop losing sales to the dealer who responded first.

Key benefits and features:
- One inbox: leads from Gmail, CarGurus, AutoTrader, website, texts, and calls land in one list.
- Each lead is tied to the exact vehicle they asked about; reply with the right link and details.
- Today list: new leads, callbacks, follow-ups, and appointments ranked by urgency.
- SMS templates with placeholders (name, vehicle, price) so messages feel personal without typing from scratch.
- Two-way texting from a business number; all threads live in the app.
- AI voice agent option for after-hours calls.
- Response-time tracking and analytics (leads by source, funnel, SMS reply rate, voice, revenue).
- Inventory sync from the dealer's website so the app stays current with what's for sale.
- BHPH and reporting options for dealers who need them.

Audience: Independent used-car dealers, BHPH lots, small dealerships. They are busy, often understaffed, and lose deals when they reply slow or forget to follow up.
```

## 2. Take stock of your intellectual inventory

ChatGPT (or your AI) already knows a lot about you if you’ve been using it regularly: every prompt, every business idea, every problem you’ve talked through. That data is gold. Your next lead magnet, mini course, or core pitch for DealerWyze can come straight from that history. Most people ignore it and start from scratch. Don’t.

**Prompt (paste the Application context above into the start of your message):**

```
[PASTE APPLICATION CONTEXT HERE]

Based on what you know about me from our conversations, create a complete inventory of my most valuable knowledge, skills, and unique perspectives. Organize these into 3 categories: (1) topics where I have deep expertise, (2) opinions I hold that go against conventional wisdom, and (3) recurring themes in the problems I solve. For each item, suggest one digital product format it could become, such as a lead magnet, mini course, or paid workshop. Ask for more detail if required.
```

---

## 3. Make your message relevant

A great message means nothing if nobody is looking for it. The intersection of **what you know** and **what people want** is where your business transforms. Find the advantage in that overlap. Stop guessing what dealers need.

**Prompt (paste the Application context at the start):**

```
[PASTE APPLICATION CONTEXT HERE]

Based on the intellectual inventory you just created, research current trends and search demand for each topic area. Identify 5 specific problems that real people (used-car dealers, BHPH operators, small dealerships) are actively searching for solutions to right now, that align with my expertise and with what DealerWyze does. For each one, explain the demand signal, why my perspective is different from what's already out there, and suggest a compelling angle I could own. Rank them by potential impact.
```

---

## 4. Write copy that makes people feel something

Generic copy is everywhere. LLMs made it easy to produce. But the words themselves don’t matter. **What matters is how you make people feel.** When a dealer reads your message and feels seen, recognized, and valued, they believe you can help. They believe you can ease their fears and deliver their goals—then they buy. Get an edit of your wording through that lens.

**Prompt (paste the Application context at the start):**

```
[PASTE APPLICATION CONTEXT HERE]

Take the top-ranked message from the previous response and write 3 versions of sales copy for it. Each version should be 150 words maximum. The copy must make the reader feel seen and understood within the first sentence. Use the problem, agitation, solution framework. Write at an 8th grade reading level. Avoid generic phrases and corporate language. Make every line feel like I'm talking directly to one person who needs this message right now.
```

---

## 5. Optimize for clarity

If you start explaining what you do and people glaze over, you have a messaging problem. Complexity kills sales. The clearest communicator wins. Distill your message into the simplest version you can say out loud without stumbling. Try a few versions. Read them out loud. See how they land.

**Prompt (paste the Application context at the start):**

```
[PASTE APPLICATION CONTEXT HERE]

Take the winning copy from the previous step and distill my core message into 5 progressively simpler versions. Start with a 30 word version, then 20, then 15, then 10, then 5. For each version, explain what was removed and why. Then suggest which single version I should use as my primary tagline, my email subject line, and my social media bio. Give me the exact words for each one.
```

---

## 6. Take the pressure off

If you’re frantically searching for your million-dollar message, you might be looking too inward and missing the obvious signs around you. The best messages show up when you stop forcing them. You already have what you need. Your ace cards are in your hand.

**Prompt (paste the Application context at the start):**

```
[PASTE APPLICATION CONTEXT HERE]

Create a guided visualization for me. Write it in second person, present tense. The scenario is this: I have found my million-dollar message. I have full alignment with my audience and my product (DealerWyze). Money is flowing to me with ease. My content resonates, my offers sell, and people share my work without being asked. Write this in 300 words. Make it vivid and specific to my actual business based on what you know about me and about DealerWyze. End with 3 action steps I can take today to make this vision real. Ask for more detail if required.
```
