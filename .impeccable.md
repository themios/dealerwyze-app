## Design Context

### Users
Independent used-car dealer owners and their small teams (1-5 people). Owner-operators running
1-20 vehicle lots. Practical, skeptical of enterprise software, working on mobile during an active
selling day. They are not tech-forward - they want a tool that works and gets out of the way.
Context: on the lot, between customers, checking leads on a phone in the sun.

### Brand Personality
**Premium, practical, direct.**

Not startup-cute. Not enterprise-corporate. Think: a well-made tool. The product of someone
who has been on the lot and built exactly what they needed - no more, no less. It earns its
price by being reliable and fast, not by looking impressive.

### Aesthetic Direction
Authoritative light mode. Navy (#0D2B55) as the dominant structural color. Orange (#F07018)
as a sharp, earned accent - not decoration, but signal. Warm off-white backgrounds (#F8F4EE,
#F4F0EA) for content sections. No gradients. No glassmorphism. No emoji icons.

**Type direction:** Barlow Semi Condensed (headings) + Archivo (body). Barlow has a
slightly industrial, no-nonsense authority - like a manual from someone who knows what
they're doing. Archivo is clean, precise, readable at any size. Neither is overused.

**NOT:** startup-friendly rounded softness, generic SaaS blue-purple palettes, anything
that looks like it was made with a template builder.

### Design Principles
1. **Earn the accent.** Orange only appears where it deserves attention. Overuse kills it.
2. **Show, don't decorate.** UI mockups and real data over abstract icons and emojis.
3. **Break the grid with purpose.** Not every section is a card grid. Vary layouts intentionally.
4. **Type does the heavy lifting.** Big, confident headings with clear hierarchy. No decoration needed.
5. **Sections must earn their place.** Long landing pages with 14 identical sections feel like AI output.
   Every section should have a different visual format and a clear job in the narrative.
